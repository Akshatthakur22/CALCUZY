About American Last Names

American last names reflect the rich cultural heritage and diverse history of the United States. Our comprehensive list of 100+ American surnames includes traditional family names, modern variations, and unique options that celebrate American identity and ancestry.

The most common American last names often have English, Scottish, Irish, or German origins, reflecting the early settlers who shaped the nation. Names like Smith, Johnson, and Williams have remained popular for generations, while newer additions reflect America's growing diversity and multicultural heritage.

When selecting an American last name, consider its historical significance, cultural meaning, and how it represents your family's story. Many American surnames carry fascinating histories tied to immigration, settlement patterns, and the American experience of building new lives in a new land.

American Last Name Origins

  • English: Smith, Johnson, Williams, Brown, Jones
  • Scottish: Campbell, Stewart, Robertson, Anderson
  • Irish: Murphy, Kelly, O'Brien, Ryan
  • German: Miller, Wagner, Schmidt, Becker
  • Italian: Rossi, Romano, Ferrari, Bianchi
  • Spanish: Garcia, Rodriguez, Martinez, Lopez

Frequently Asked Questions

How many American last names are in the list?

Our comprehensive list includes over 100 American last names, divided into common, traditional, and modern categories. This provides plenty of options for finding the perfect surname for your family.

What are the most common American last names?

The most common American last names include Smith, Johnson, Williams, Brown, and Jones. These names have remained popular for decades and reflect the English heritage of many early American settlers.

What origins do American last names have?

American last names come from diverse origins including English, Scottish, Irish, German, Italian, Spanish, and many other European and international backgrounds. This diversity reflects America's multicultural heritage and immigration history.

How do I research my American last name?

Research your American last name through genealogy websites, census records, immigration documents, and family histories. Many American surnames have fascinating stories tied to your ancestors' journey to America.

Can I change my American last name?

Yes, you can legally change your American last name through court petition. The process typically involves filing paperwork, publishing a notice, and attending a court hearing. Many people change names for marriage, divorce, or personal preference.
